#Agriculture irrigation

Agriculture irrigation is the process of artificially applying water to land to assist in the growth of crops, landscapes, and lawns. It's a crucial practice in regions with insufficient or unreliable rainfall, ensuring crops receive the necessary water for survival and growth. Irrigation systems utilize various methods to deliver water, including surface irrigation (flooding), sprinkler irrigation, drip irrigation, and sub-surface irrigation, each with its own advantages and disadvantages.

Why is Irrigation Important?

Crop Growth: Plants need water for photosynthesis and nutrient uptake, and irrigation ensures they receive enough.
